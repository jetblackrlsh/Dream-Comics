# Treatment

## Logline

Lucid after sleep paralysis, Jet enters Anywhere City Mall with unlimited funds and discovers that buying everything may be the one purchase that makes everything disappear.

## Central Conflict

Jet can buy anything. The conflict is not scarcity, but too much possibility: unlimited funds create choice anxiety, ethical pressure, and a magical marketplace paradox. If the only mall contains every item currently for sale, buying the whole mall may stop everything from being for sale, which could erase the very abundance Jet is trying to possess.

## Theme

Unlimited access does not remove responsibility. Possibility has to remain alive, circulating, and available, or it collapses into possession.

## Emotional Arc

Jet begins with the thrill of absolute purchasing power. The mall overwhelms him with infinite options. He considers buying everything as a shortcut around choice, then realizes the act would deprive the entire city. The idea deepens into a magical paradox: owning all options may destroy access to them. By the end, Jet buys nothing, but gains something more valuable: a lucid insight about desire, fairness, and systems.

## Ending

Jet stands at the checkout counter with the black card unused. Around him, the mall remains stocked, alive, and open to everyone. The final image resolves the dream visually: the black card floats beside a glowing paradox loop, and every possible item remains just out of possession but still available.
