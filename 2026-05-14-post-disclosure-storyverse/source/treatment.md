# Treatment

## Title

05/14/2026: Post-Disclosure Storyverse

## Logline

After inducing lucidity through sleep paralysis, Jet spawns alone in a Storyverse world transformed by UAP disclosure, where politicians use cosmic uncertainty to push religious law while independent builders race to recreate alien technology.

## Central Conflict

Jet wants to understand whether disclosure has created the dystopia he fears: a society where religious interpretation becomes state power. Instead, he finds a subtler contradiction. Public life is covered in religious symbols and political certainty, but everyday life still functions, while indie technologists turn alien knowledge into a wave of invention.

## Theme

Mystery can inspire discovery or be weaponized into control. Disclosure should expand human imagination without narrowing freedom of belief.

## Ending

Jet watches a backyard alien-tech prototype rise above a skyline of crosses, civic buildings, and ordinary neighborhoods. He leaves with a guarded hope that the waking world can pursue new technology without surrendering religious freedom or cultural identity.

## Emotional Arc

Jet begins wary and defensive, expecting a harsh theocratic dystopia. He becomes unsettled when politicians frame UAP as proof of their dogma, then more reflective when the world remains familiar despite the pressure. His curiosity returns when he discovers an expanded builder culture, and he ends thoughtful rather than triumphant, carrying a warning and a hope.

## Page Count

Eight pages: cover plus seven story pages.
